Introduction

Before I Knew What I Was Protecting

I graduated in International Marketing with the kind of confidence that only theory gives you.

Later I added Digital Marketing because it felt like the future. Data,automation,funnels,positioning. I believed systems could be improved. I believed companies were inefficient only because no one had thought clearly enough.

At twenty-four,I was convinced that clarity was rare and that I had it.

Then I met someone who wasn’t impressed by frameworks.

She was drawn to energy,not strategy. Presence,not projection.

And I was drawn to the idea that I could finally become someone who didn’t need to prove anything.

But success didn’t remove the mask. It upgraded it.

Recognition didn’t calm me. It gave me an audience.

And that audience started to shape what I could admit,even to myself.

One night,she asked a question that should have been simple.
